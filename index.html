Welcome to the Playbook

Changing job families is more than a structural shift; it's a human experience. This guide empowers you to lead this change effectively, empathetically, and with confidence.

Top Tip

Always start with the 'Why'.

Our Guiding Principles

Three core themes anchor our approach to every job family change.

Employee-Centric

Every decision is viewed through the lens of the employee experience. We prioritize clarity, support, and two-way communication.

Key Action: Host "Ask Me Anything" sessions with leadership.
  • Proactive career pathing
  • Personalized impact statements

Manager Empowerment

Managers are the most critical link. We equip them with the tools, training, and confidence to lead their teams through the transition.

Key Action: Provide managers with a dedicated FAQ and talking points one week before employees are told.
  • Just-in-time training modules
  • 1:1 coaching from HRBPs

Data-Informed

We use data to guide our planning and measure success, from initial sentiment analysis to post-launch productivity metrics.

Key Action: Launch a pulse survey 30 days after the change to measure sentiment.
  • Pre-change readiness scores
  • Post-change engagement tracking

The 5-Phase Process

Follow this structured process for a predictable and successful outcome.

Define the Scope

Articulate the business case and define which roles are in scope.

Remember: A clear problem statement is half the solution.

Identify Stakeholders

Map out all key stakeholders, from sponsors to impacted employees.

Tip: Don't forget adjacent teams who might be affected.

Map New Job Families

Develop the new job descriptions, leveling guides, and career paths.

Tip: Involve senior ICs in the design process for better buy-in.

Conduct Impact Analysis

Analyze potential impacts on compensation, career, and teams.

Remember: Be prepared to address compensation questions head-on.
